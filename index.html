Case Overview

Allegation

The TownCrier ("Doesn't add UP!") investigation asserts that Plantation at Leesburg HOA governance and accounting contain material irregularities involving:

  • An authorized $42,300/month draw to support golf operations that may have been misclassified, partially omitted from the Balance Sheet, and accompanied by an alleged January 2025 excess draw of ~$50,000.
  • A fabricated December 2025 year-end financial package, including a reported $4,876,898.14 Total Reserve Funding line that appears implausible relative to monthly norms.
  • A December 2025 year-end balance sheet that does not balance (Total Assets $1,838,775.84 vs Total Liabilities & Equity $5,122,035.42).
  • A phantom $453,129.67 profit posted in December 2025 and deleted in January 2026.
  • An effective collapse of HOA equity from ~$7,767,960 (FY2024 audited) to $48,692 (April 2026).
  • Claims that SPA 2021 revenue vanishes from January–March 2026 statements despite continued collections.
  • Active litigation including a special-assessment lawsuit and Plantation of Leesburg HOA v. Wolner (5D2023-2882) arising from a receivership of a hospitalized resident.

Confirmed Findings

F1 — $42,300/month authorized golf draw confirmed

golf-course-llc.txt and monthly HOA statements document an authorized $42,300/month Special Activity draw. FY2024 aggregate transfer of $695,271.77 is also documented. This is corroborated, not disputed.

F2 — Phantom $453,129.67 profit: posted then deleted across Dec 2025 / Jan 2026

The December 2025 balance sheet shows a distinct Total Net Income / (Loss) … $ 453,129.67 line. The January 2026 balance sheet shows this line at $207.58 then $50.00. The entry disappears entirely between year-end and January 1, 2026 — a literal match to the alleged sequence.

F3 — December 2025 year-end financials are mechanically implausible

December 2025 consolidated income statement reports Total Revenue of only $37,746.30 against a ~$450K–$507K monthly run-rate. Total Reserve Funding shows a single $4,876,898.14 line with no movement pattern. Utilities appear only in the total column with no Operating/SA breakdown. These are not credible outputs from a functioning accounting system.

F4 — December 2025 balance sheet does not balance

From the December 2025 balance sheet: Total Assets = $1,838,775.84; Total Liabilities & Equity = $5,122,035.42. A $3.28M difference that violates the fundamental accounting equation.

F5 — Effective equity collapse 2024 to 2026

FY2024 audited balance sheet: Total Equity = $7,767,959.65. April 2026 consolidated balance sheet: Total Equity = $48,691.69. Equity depletion of approximately $7.72M over roughly two years.

F6 — Widespread monthly statement integrity failures

January 2026 consolidated income statement: Operating $9,879.81 + Special Assessment $17,201.68 ≠ Total $933.05 — arithmetic impossible. Multiple months show single-digit or triple-dollar operating results against ~$500K revenue run-rates. These are characteristic of retroactive modification or templating errors, not normal period-close output.

Governing Statute / Compliance

Statutory Framework — Florida Homeowners' Association Act (Ch. 720, Fla. Stat.) and Related Laws

The TownCrier findings are assessed against the following regulatory baseline:

  • Chapter 720, Florida Statutes (Homeowners' Association Act) — Primary authority governing daily operations, board duties, and dispute resolution. Key provisions include:
    • Record retention/access requirements for financial statements, budgets, and official records.
    • Meeting openness and notice requirements for board and membership meetings.
    • Special-assessment limitations, voting thresholds, and lien collection procedures.
    • Audit and financial reporting obligations; reserve study/update requirements.
    • Fiduciary duties, conflict-of-interest standards, and enforcement powers.
  • HB 1203 / digital portal mandates — Requires public posting/secure portal for 100+ parcel associations.
  • Chapter 617, Florida Statutes — Not-for-profit corporation duties; applicable because the association is a Florida nonprofit.
  • Chapter 468, Part VIII — Community association manager licensing and conflict-of-interest disclosure.
  • FS 812.014 — Criminal theft; applies to unauthorized use of HOA debit cards or funds.
  • FS 837 / civil remedies — False claims / malicious prosecution exposure for knowingly false litigation; referenced in TownCrier complaint context.
  • Florida Notary / attestation rules — Governing instrument execution; may affect enforceability if notices/liens lack proper attestation.

How the Bylaws Relate to the Allegations

  • Board removal / turnover rules (Art. V, VI) — Class B declarant control ends at turnover or 95% conveyance. After turnover, directors must be members; proxies and officer duties are formally defined. Documentation should confirm whether post-turnover board composition and elections comply.
  • Meetings and notices (Art. IV, §§ 1–7) — Annual and special meetings, quorum, 5–30-day notice, quorum waivers. Alleged document-access delays and comment restrictions are relevant to whether the board is consistent with notice and member participation.
  • Assessments and liens (Art. VII) — Annual, special, and individual assessments; notice requirements; 30-day delinquency -> lien; subordinate to mortgages. The TownCrier narrative disputes classification and treatment of special assessments and golf draw funding; bylaws govern what the association may assess and how delinquency and lien enforcement should operate.
  • Compliance/default (Art. VIII) — Written notice of breach, 30-day cure, then legal/equitable relief; no waiver of future rights. If obligations under the declaration or rules are being selectively enforced, this article defines the proper procedure.
  • Amendments (Art. IX) — Pre-turnover by board; post-turnover by membership with 75% vote. Amendments affecting assessments or governance should be checked for procedural compliance.
  • Fiduciary overlays — The board’s statutory duties under § 720.305 and § 617.0830 apply in addition to these bylaws; reserve withdrawals, transfers to the golf LLC, and financial reporting fall in this overlap zone.
