Case Overview

Allegation

TownCryer.biz published claims that the Plantation at Leesburg HOA authorized a $42,300/month golf-course draw but systematically omitted those transfers from the HOA Balance Sheet and misclassified them as LLC revenue. The article specifically alleged:

  • An excess draw of $92,300 for January 2025 (versus an authorized $42,300)
  • Total identified irregularities of $136,522

The hypothesis under review was that these figures represented actual financial irregularities supported by the HOA ledger.

Findings of Fact

Fact 1 — Routine Special Activity Draw

The HOA budgeting and accounting system includes account 400832 Special Activity Income, which is used to record the monthly draw to support golf operations. This account appears consistently across multiple fiscal periods and is treated as a budgeted contra-revenue item.

Fact 2 — Verified Draw Amount

For the period January 2025 through April 2025, the actual monthly postings equal the budgeted amount exactly:

  • January 2025: $(42,300.00) actual = $(42,300.00) budget; variance $0
  • February 2025: $(42,300.00) actual = $(42,300.00) budget; variance $0
  • March 2025: $(42,300.00) actual = $(42,300.00) budget; variance $0
  • April 2025: $(42,300.00) actual = $(42,300.00) budget; variance $0

Year-to-date actuals also match budget exactly through each month. There is no evidence of an additional $50,000 monthly excess in any extracted ledger.

Fact 3 — No $92,300 or $136,522 in Extracted Ledger

A comprehensive search of D:\HOA\PLA_Financials_extracted\ found no file containing the figures $92,300 or $136,522. The only draw-related amounts are the $42,300 monthly entries and related budget/actual line items.

Fact 4 — Separate Contra-Revenue Account

Account 400109 Contra Revenue - Golf Course Allocation exists as a distinct line item from 400832 Special Activity Income. In January–March 2025, this account was blank; in April 2025 it showed $(209.00). These values are not the draw amount, nor do they combine to $92,300.

Fact 5 — FY2024 Transfer Total Corroborates Structured Transfers

Entity files note FY2024 transfers totaling $695,271.77. This figure is documented as part of the operating structure, not as an irregular undisclosed draw. It does not support the $92,300 or $136,522 claim.

Legal Analysis

Corroboration vs. Proof

The available extracted documents corroborate the existence of a budgeted, structured $42,300/month Special Activity draw. They do not corroborate an additional irregular $50,000 monthly excess, nor a $136,522 cumulative anomaly. The variance column in every monthly statement is zero for this line item, indicating full compliance with the approved budget.

Recharacterization Not Supported

The claim that transfers were misclassified as LLC revenue is not borne out by the extracted statements. 400832 Special Activity Income is recorded as a contra-revenue item in the HOA’s books. The existence of a separate golf course entity and related advances does not, without additional documentation, establish intentional misclassification or fraud.

Burden of Proof

The party advancing the hypothesis must produce primary source documents that reconcile to the same ledger. A blog allegation without exhibits linking to the actual monthly statements, or without a consolidated statement combining 400832, 400109, and related due-to/from lines, does not meet the evidentiary threshold to alter the determination.

Conclusion

Determination
REFUTED
The hypothesis is unsupported by the financial evidence reviewed.

Reasoning

The extracted ledger documents demonstrate that the $42,300 monthly Special Activity draw is a budgeted, authorized line item with zero variance for January–April 2025. No extracted file contains the $92,300 or $136,522 figures alleged. Without the exact TownCryer.biz article URL and cited exhibits that reconcile to this ledger, the hypothesis cannot be sustained.
