PROXY VOTE EXPLAINER (plain English)
================================================================================

HOW TO VOTE BY PROXY
Plantation at Leesburg HOA

WHAT IS A PROXY?
A proxy is a legal form that lets you give someone else permission to vote on your behalf at the annual meeting. It's useful when you:
- Are traveling or out of town
- Have a scheduling conflict
- Prefer not to attend in person

YOUR RIGHTS:
- You can authorize any adult resident or member to vote on your behalf
- You can give specific instructions (e.g., vote for [Candidate])
- If the form doesn't say how to vote, the proxy holder may vote as they choose - so be clear!

HOW TO COMPLETE YOUR PROXY:
1. Obtain the official HOA proxy form from:
   - HOA office (25201 US Hwy 27, Leesburg, FL 34748)
   - [Name] campaign (contact below if you need assistance)
2. Fill in:
   - Your name and parcel/home address
   - Name of the person you authorize
   - Date
   - Your signature
3. [Required by your bylaws: Notarization? Witness? Check rules.]
4. Return the completed form before the deadline to:
   - HOA office
   - Designated drop-off location
   - Candidate/trusted neighbor collecting proxies (if allowed)

TIPS:
- Complete your proxy as early as possible. Last-minute proxies can be invalid if the board receives them after the deadline.
- Keep a copy for your records.
- If you're unsure about any part of the form, ask [Name] or contact the HOA office.

QUESTIONS? Contact [Name] at [phone/email]. I can provide blank proxy forms and read through them with you.

Note: This is not legal advice. Consult your HOA governing documents for exact requirements, or ask a Florida attorney licensed in homeowners association law.
