How to Vote by Proxy

What is a proxy? A legal form authorizing another person to vote on your behalf at the annual meeting. Use it if you’re traveling, have a conflict, or can’t attend.

  • You can authorize any adult resident or member.
  • Be specific if you want your proxy to vote a certain way.
  • If the form is silent, the proxy holder may vote as they choose.

Steps: 1) Get the official HOA proxy form at the office (25201 US Hwy 27, Leesburg, FL 34748) or from the campaign. 2) Complete owner name, parcel, proxy name, date, and signature. 3) Confirm notarization/witness requirements with the HOA office. 4) Return before deadline.

Election Compliance Checklist

  • Notice period — Follow declaration/bylaws for annual meeting notice (typically 14 days; confirm exact rule in your governing docs).
  • Proxy form requirements — Confirm whether bylaws require notarization, witness, or specific form language.
  • Quorum — Confirm quorum requirement so you know the minimum attendance threshold.
  • Electioneering — Do not campaign in HOA-controlled channels (newsletters, official social pages). Stick to personal outreach, door-knocking, and independent channels.
  • Candidate statement — Check for word limits if the board publishes ballot statements.
  • Candidate registration/filing deadline — Identify exact cutoff from the annual meeting notice.
  • Voter rolls — Verify your contact list against the official owner/parcel roster.
Compliance Notes

Florida Statute Quick Reference (Campaign-safe)

  • § 720.303(2) — Open meetings; 14-day notice for certain votes; 3-minute speaking rights.
  • § 720.303(4)/(8) — Records access and website posting for 100+ parcel associations.
  • § 720.303(6)/(7) — Annual audit when revenues ≥ $500K.
  • § 720.305 — Fine/suspension due process: 14-day written notice, independent hearing committee, 90-day window.
  • § 720.304 / 720.3045 — Property rights protections (holiday decor, trash cans, artificial turf, etc.).
  • § 720.309 — Special assessment notice/vote requirements.
  • § 720.3033 — Board education: 4-hour curriculum within 90 days of taking office.
  • FS 812.014 — Unauthorized use of association debit cards = theft.
